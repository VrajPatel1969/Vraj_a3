Northern White Rhino
Ceratotherium simum cottoni

The northern white rhino is a large, herbivorous mammal that was once widespread across central and eastern Africa. However, due to poaching for their horns and habitat loss, the subspecies has suffered a drastic decline in population. In the 1960s, there were around 2,000 northern white rhinos, but by 2008, only four individuals remained, all in captivity.
Since then, the subspecies has been considered functionally extinct, as both males have died, leaving only two females alive, Najin and Fatu, both living under 24-hour guard at Ol Pejeta Conservancy in Kenya.

Information On Northern White Rhinos

Interesting FactS

Fact 1

Only two northern white rhinos remain in the world: There are only two known northern white rhinos remaining in the world, both of which are female. The last male northern white rhino, named Sudan, died in 2018, leaving behind only his daughter and granddaughter.

Fact 2

Poaching is the primary reason for their decline: Northern white rhinos were once widespread across central and east Africa, but their populations were decimated by poaching for their horns, which are believed to have medicinal properties in some cultures. As a result, they are now critically endangered and on the brink of extinction.

Fact 3

Efforts are underway to save the species through in vitro fertilization: Conservationists are working to save the northern white rhino from extinction through the use of in vitro fertilization (IVF) techniques. Scientists have collected and stored genetic material from several northern white rhinos, and hope to use this material to create embryos that can be implanted in surrogate southern white rhinos, which are more numerous and easier to breed in captivity.

Cause of Poaching

Demand for their horns

The primary reason for poaching of northern white rhinos is the demand for their horns. Rhino horns are highly valued in some cultures for their supposed medicinal properties and as a status symbol. Despite there being no scientific evidence to support these beliefs, the demand for rhino horns remains high.

Weak law enforcement

Another reason for the poaching of northern white rhinos is the weak law enforcement in some countries where they are found. In many cases, poachers can operate with impunity due to inadequate resources and corruption within law enforcement agencies. This makes it easier for them to kill rhinos and smuggle their horns out of the country.

Poverty and economic incentives:

In some areas where northern white rhinos are found, there may be few economic opportunities for local people, leading them to turn to poaching as a means of making a living. This is especially true in countries where the black market trade in rhino horns can be highly profitable. Some poachers may also be motivated by criminal organizations that offer them financial incentives to carry out poaching activities.
